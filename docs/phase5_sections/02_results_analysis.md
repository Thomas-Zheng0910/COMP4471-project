## 2.1 Final-epoch quantitative comparison (epoch 15)

From `v2_fullscale_comparison_summary.json`:

| Method | Train final | Val final | Generalization gap | Val RMSE | Val AbsRel |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| RGB-only | 0.6454 | 1.4369 | 0.7915 | 0.5607 | 0.1421 |
| Supervision-only | 0.6581 | 1.5004 | 0.8423 | 0.5618 | 0.1409 |
| Late fusion | 0.6155 | 1.2554 | 0.6399 | **0.3841** | 0.09078 |
| Token fusion | **0.6092** | **1.2356** | **0.6264** | 0.3878 | **0.09018** |

### Relative change vs RGB-only (computed from summary JSON)

- **Supervision-only**:
  - RMSE: -0.20% (slightly worse)
  - AbsRel: +0.82% (slightly better)
  - Val loss: -4.42% (worse)
  - Gap reduction: -6.42% (worse)

- **Late fusion**:
  - RMSE: +31.49% improvement
  - AbsRel: +36.12% improvement
  - Val loss: +12.63% improvement
  - Gap reduction: +19.15%

- **Token fusion**:
  - RMSE: +30.84% improvement
  - AbsRel: +36.54% improvement
  - Val loss: +14.01% improvement
  - Gap reduction: +20.86%

### Token vs Late (trade-off)

- Token has slightly better final `val_loss` (1.2356 vs 1.2554) and lower final gap.
- Late has slightly better final RMSE (0.3841 vs 0.3878).
- Token has slightly better final AbsRel (0.09018 vs 0.09078).

Interpretation: both fusion methods are clearly superior to non-fusion baselines; token and late are very close, with complementary strengths.

## 2.2 Figure-by-figure interpretation

## A) `v2_training_error_lineplot.png`

Expected pattern from metric history:

- All methods show monotonic or near-monotonic training loss decrease.
- Final ordering (best to worst train loss): Token ≈ Late < RGB-only < Supervision-only.

Implication: token/late fusion do not hurt optimization stability; they improve fitting quality even on training data.

## B) `v2_testing_error_lineplot.png`

Expected pattern:

- Fusion curves (late/token) stay consistently below RGB-only and supervision-only after early epochs.
- Token reaches the best final test loss among all methods.

Implication: gains are not only optimization-side; they transfer to held-out split metrics.

## C) `v2_generalization_gap_lineplot.png`

Definition in script: `generalization_gap = val_loss - train_loss`.

Observed from history JSON:

- Gap rises with epochs for all methods (common in this training regime).
- Final gap is smallest for Token, second smallest for Late.

Implication: fusion methods appear to improve train-to-val consistency relative to non-fusion baselines.

## D) `v2_final_error_bar.png`

This bar chart compares final train/test losses together.

- Fusion methods have lower final train and lower final test losses.
- Non-fusion methods show larger train-test separation.

Implication: visually confirms gap and final-error advantages of LiDAR feature fusion.

## 2.4 Key scientific conclusions from this result set

1. **LiDAR supervision alone is insufficient** in this setup (supervision-only does not improve RMSE over RGB-only).
2. **LiDAR feature fusion is the dominant gain source** (late/token produce large RMSE and AbsRel improvements).
3. **Multi-scale token fusion and single-scale late fusion are both strong**, with small metric-dependent differences.
4. **For paper reporting**, choose primary ranking metric explicitly:
   - RMSE primary -> Late best by a small margin.
   - AbsRel or final val loss primary -> Token best by a small margin.
